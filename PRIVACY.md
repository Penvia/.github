## PRIVACY POLICY
**Effective Date:** April 26, 2026

---

This Privacy Policy (the **"Policy"**) describes the privacy practices of **Vulpi** (hereinafter referred to as the **"Developer"**) regarding the software product **Penvia**, including its Client application and Desktop components (collectively, the **"Software"**).

### 1. DATA COLLECTION AND USE
The Developer is committed to a "Privacy by Design" philosophy. 
* **Zero Data Collection:** The Developer does **not** collect, store, or transmit any personal data or usage analytics. 
* **Local Processing:** All input data (including stylus coordinates, pressure, and tilt) is processed locally on your devices and transmitted directly between your Android device and your Desktop server. This data is never routed through, stored on, or accessible by the Developer's infrastructure.
* **Update Checks:** To ensure security and performance, the Software periodically checks for new versions. The server uses Velopack for automatic updates, while the Client application performs a manual version check. These checks involve communicating with GitHub.com, which may process technical identifiers (such as an IP address) to deliver the update metadata. This data is handled by GitHub according to their privacy policy and is never collected, stored, or accessed by the Developer.

### 2. NETWORK TRANSMISSION
To facilitate the "bridge" functionality, the Software requires access to your local network.
* **Point-to-Point:** Data transmission is intended for local networks (Wi-Fi or USB). 
* **Third-Party Hosting:** If you choose to connect the Software to a server hosted by a third party, you acknowledge that the Developer has no control over, and assumes no responsibility for, the data handling practices of that third party.

### 3. CHANGES TO THIS POLICY
The Developer reserves the right to modify this Policy at any time to reflect changes in the Software's functionality. Your continued use of the Software following any such changes constitutes your acceptance of the updated Policy.
